TRAVEL ACROSS JAPAN

Tokyo

Tokyo is a city of contrasts, where neon-lit skyscrapers tower above centuries-old shrines and bustling markets. Dive into the electric energy of Shibuya Crossing, then slip into quiet alleyways for hidden izakayas and ramen counters. A must-do experience here is visiting the buzzing Tsukiji Outer Market, where you can taste the freshest sushi and street snacks while soaking in the city’s unstoppable rhythm. Every corner feels like a new world; futuristic, chaotic, and irresistibly alive. For a perfect city escape, stroll through the peaceful gardens of the Imperial Palace.

Kyoto

Kyoto is Japan’s timeless heart, where ancient temples, tranquil gardens, and lantern-lit alleyways blend seamlessly with modern life. A must-do experience here is exploring the enchanting Fushimi Inari Shrine, famous for its endless rows of vermilion torii gates that wind up the sacred Mount Inari. Walking through this glowing tunnel feels like stepping into another world—equal parts spiritual journey. Whether you linger in the serene hush of the forested trails or capture the iconic gates at golden hour, this is the essence of Kyoto you’ll never forget.

Nara

Nara is where Japan’s ancient spirit comes alive, blending vast parks, wooden temples, and free-roaming deer. The city’s star attraction is Todai-ji Temple, home to the Great Buddha, an awe-inspiring bronze giant housed in the world’s largest wooden hall. Beyond the temple, deer wander through Nara Park, bowing politely (and sometimes cheekily demanding snacks) for crackers. The mix of sacred calm and playful wildlife makes Nara unforgettable. It’s an easy day trip from Kyoto, yet it feels like stepping centuries back in time.

Hiroshima

Hiroshima is a city of resilience, rebuilt from tragedy into a vibrant, forward-looking hub. The Peace Memorial Park and Museum is a moving reminder of history, offering a sobering yet essential experience. But Hiroshima is not only about remembrance—it’s also alive with riverside walks, bustling nightlife, and hearty local food. Be sure to try Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki, a layered pancake dish grilled right in front of you. Few cities balance sorrow and hope with such grace. Take a quiet moment at the Atomic Bomb Dome, where history stands in powerful silence.

Miyajima

Just a short ferry ride from Hiroshima lies Miyajima, a small island that feels almost mythical. Its centerpiece is the floating torii gate of Itsukushima Shrine, which seems to rise straight out of the sea at high tide. Beyond the iconic view, the island offers forested hiking trails up Mount Misen, with sweeping panoramas across the Inland Sea. Street stalls tempt you with grilled oysters and maple-leaf shaped cakes, local specialties. Miyajima radiates a calm, otherworldly beauty that lingers long after you leave. Stay past dusk to see the torii gate illuminated.

Osaka

Osaka is Japan’s kitchen—loud, colorful, and full of flavor. The neon-drenched streets of Dotonbori are the perfect stage to feast on takoyaki, skewers, and fluffy okonomiyaki straight from sizzling griddles. The city has a playful, welcoming vibe, less polished than Tokyo but equally magnetic. By day, explore Osaka Castle and riverside parks; by night, dive into the buzzing nightlife where laughter spills into the streets. Cap it off with a ride on the giant Tempozan Ferris Wheel. Osaka is the place to eat, drink, and let loose before your journey ends.
